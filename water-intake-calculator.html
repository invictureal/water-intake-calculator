How your daily water intake is calculated

The calculator starts from a baseline of 35 ml/kg for under 18, 33 ml/kg for 18–65, and 30 ml/kg for over 65. It then adds adjustments for activity level (up to +1000 ml), weather (up to +750 ml), and health status (pregnancy, breastfeeding, or fever). Height contributes a small body-surface-area correction, since larger-framed bodies lose slightly more fluid through skin and breath at the same weight. This is a general wellness estimate, not medical advice — anyone managing a kidney, heart, or liver condition should follow their doctor's fluid guidance.

Frequently asked questions

How much water should I drink a day?

Most adults need roughly 30–35 ml per kilogram of body weight daily, adjusted upward for activity, heat, pregnancy, breastfeeding, or illness. Use the calculator above for a number tailored to you.

Does coffee or tea count toward water intake?

Yes, in moderation. Caffeinated drinks still contribute fluid, though plain water remains the best default choice.

Is this a substitute for medical advice?

No. It's a general wellness estimate. Anyone with a kidney, heart, or liver condition should follow their doctor's fluid guidance instead.

Why does it ask for my height?

Height combines with weight to estimate body surface area, which has a small effect on fluid loss through skin and breathing — a minor adjustment next to activity and weather.
